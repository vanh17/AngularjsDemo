Financial Market Today

U.S. stock index futures indicated a higher open on Thursday as traders eyed a meeting of the European Central Bank in Frankfurt, Germany.The ECB is expected to cut its deposit rate further into negative territory and boost asset purchases in an effort to spark euro zone inflation and encourage growth.

On the U.S. data front, initial claims are due at 8:30 a.m. ET, with the Federal budget released at 2:00 p.m.On the earnings front, Dollar General (NYSE: DG), Party City (NYSE: PRTY), Vail Resorts (NYSE: MTN) and El Pollo Loco (NASDAQ: LOCO) are among companies set to report today.In oil markets, Brent crude traded at around $40.60 a barrel, down 1.14 percent, while U.S. crude was at around $37.88, down 1.07 percent.

In Asia, Japan's Nikkei (Nihon Kenzai Shinbun: .N225)closed up 1.26 percent, while the Shanghai Composite (Shanghai Stock Exchange: .SSEC) in China closed 2.03 percent lower.

In Europe, the pan European Stoxx 600 Index (STOXX: .STOXX) was up by around 0.07 percent on Thursday morning.

CNBC's Patti Domm contributed to this report
